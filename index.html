Dill

Dill, scientifically known as Anethum graveolens, is an annual herb from the celery family (Apiaceae) cultivated for its leaves and seeds, which are used as a herb or spice. It's known for its distinct flavor profile, often described as a combination of fennel, celery, and anise with slightly bitter undertones. .

chives

Chives are a perennial herb with long, slender, green leaves and purple-pink flowers. They belong to the onion family (Allium) and are known for their mild, onion-like flavor. Chives are used as a culinary herb and are also a good source of vitamins A and C, as well as other nutrients like calcium, iron, and potassium.
